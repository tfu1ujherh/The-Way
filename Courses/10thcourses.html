Physics (Class 11 & 12 Syllabus)

Physics is a fundamental subject that deals with the laws of nature and the properties of matter and energy.

Class 11 Physics Syllabus:

    Physical World and Measurement: Units, dimensions, errors, and the basics of physical quantities.

    Kinematics: Motion in a straight line, relative motion, velocity, and acceleration.

    Laws of Motion: Newton’s laws, momentum, friction, and equilibrium.

    Work, Energy, and Power: Concepts of work, energy, and power, kinetic and potential energy.

    System of Particles and Rotational Motion: Rigid bodies, rotational dynamics, and moment of inertia.

    Gravitation: Universal law of gravitation, planetary motion, and satellites.

    Properties of Bulk Matter: Elasticity, pressure, surface tension, and viscosity.

    Thermodynamics: Laws of thermodynamics, heat transfer, and kinetic theory of gases.

    Oscillations and Waves: Simple harmonic motion, wave motion, and sound waves.

    Recommended Reference Books:

      Concepts of Physics by H.C. Verma (Volume 1 & 2)

      Problems in General Physics by I.E. Irodov

Class 12 Physics Syllabus:

    Electrostatics: Electric charges, electric fields, potential, and capacitance.

    Current Electricity: Ohm’s law, Kirchhoff’s rules, and electrical circuits.

    Magnetic Effects of Current & Magnetism: Biot-Savart law, Ampere’s law, and electromagnetic induction.

    Electromagnetic Waves: Nature of electromagnetic waves and the electromagnetic spectrum.

    Optics: Ray optics, wave optics, reflection, refraction, and interference.

    Dual Nature of Matter and Radiation: Photoelectric effect and quantum mechanics.

    Atoms and Nuclei: Atomic models, nuclear reactions, and radioactivity.

    Electronic Devices: Semiconductors, diodes, and transistors.

Chemistry (Class 11 & 12 Syllabus):

Chemistry deals with the composition, properties, and structure of substances and the changes they undergo.

Class 11 Chemistry Syllabus:

    Some Basic Concepts of Chemistry: Atomic mass, mole concept, and stoichiometry.

    Structure of Atom: Quantum numbers, electronic configuration, and atomic models.

    Chemical Bonding and Molecular Structure: Covalent, ionic bonding, and VSEPR theory.

    States of Matter: Gases, liquids, and intermolecular forces.

    Thermodynamics: First and second laws of thermodynamics, enthalpy, and entropy.

    Equilibrium: Chemical equilibrium, acids, bases, and ionic equilibrium.

    Redox Reactions: Oxidation, reduction, and balancing redox reactions.

    Organic Chemistry – Some Basic Principles and Techniques: Hybridization, classification of organic compounds, and IUPAC nomenclature.

    Recommended Reference Books:

    Electrochemistry: Electrolytes, galvanic cells, and Nernst equation.

Mathematics (Class 11 & 12 Syllabus):

Mathematics is the study of numbers, quantities, shapes, and patterns and their relationships.

Class 11 Mathematics Syllabus:

    Sets, Relations, and Functions: Basic set theory, types of relations, functions, and binary operations.

    Trigonometry: Functions, equations, and identities related to trigonometry.

    Complex Numbers and Quadratic Equations: Basic algebraic operations with complex numbers and solving quadratic equations.

    Permutations and Combinations: Fundamental principles of counting and factorial notation.

    Binomial Theorem: Expansion using Pascal’s triangle and application of binomial theorem.

    Sequences and Series: Arithmetic progression (AP) and geometric progression (GP).

    Straight Lines and Conic Sections: Equations and properties of lines, circles, parabolas, ellipses, and hyperbolas.

    Limits and Derivatives: Introduction to limits, continuity, and basic differentiation.

Recommended Reference Books:

    NCERT Mathematics (Class 11 & 12)

Accountancy (Class 11 & 12 Syllabus)

Accountancy focuses on the recording, classifying, and summarizing of financial transactions to provide insights into business performance and financial status.

Class 11 Accountancy Syllabus:

Theoretical Framework: Introduction to accounting, accounting principles, concepts, and conventions.

Accounting Process: Recording of transactions, preparation of trial balance, rectification of errors.

Bank Reconciliation Statement: Concept and preparation of a bank reconciliation statement.

Depreciation, Provisions, and Reserves: Methods of depreciation, provision for bad debts, and reserves.

Accounting for Bills of Exchange: Concept, features, and accounting treatment of bills of exchange.

Financial Statements: Preparation of final accounts including trading, profit and loss account, and balance sheet.

Accounts from Incomplete Records: Single-entry system and its conversion into the double-entry system.

Economics (Class 11 & 12 Syllabus)

Economics studies the production, distribution, and consumption of goods and services, as well as the behavior and interactions of economic agents.

Class 11 Economics Syllabus:

Microeconomics: Introduction to microeconomics, consumer behavior, demand, supply, and elasticity.

Producer Behavior and Supply: Production function, returns to a factor, and cost and revenue concepts.

Forms of Market and Price Determination: Market structures such as perfect competition, monopoly, and oligopoly.

Statistics for Economics: Introduction to statistics, measures of central tendency (mean, median, mode), and measures of dispersion.

Correlation and Index Numbers: Methods of correlation, index numbers, and their application in economics.

Political Science (Class 11 & 12 Syllabus):

Political Science explores the theory and practice of politics, governance systems, political ideologies, and the functioning of governments. It helps students understand the dynamics of power, democracy, and the role of institutions in shaping societies.

Class 11 Political Science Syllabus:

    Political Theory: Key concepts in political science such as justice, equality, liberty, and rights. Understanding democracy, citizenship, and the idea of the state.

    Indian Constitution at Work: Evolution, significance, and key features of the Indian Constitution, including the Preamble, Fundamental Rights, Directive Principles of State Policy, and Fundamental Duties.

    Election and Representation: Processes of election, representation, and the role of the Election Commission of India. Understanding first-past-the-post and proportional representation systems.

    Executive, Legislature, and Judiciary: Roles and functions of the Parliament, President, Prime Minister, and the Supreme Court in India. The separation of powers and judicial review.

    Local Governments: Panchayati Raj, Municipalities, and their importance in the decentralization of power. 73rd and 74th Constitutional Amendments.

    Federalism: Division of powers between the center and the states in India. Challenges to Indian federalism, including regionalism, language, and identity politics.

Recommended Reference Books:

    NCERT Political Science (Class 11 & 12)

    An Introduction to Political Theory by O.P. Gauba

    Indian Polity by M. Laxmikanth

    Contemporary World Politics: Cold War era, disintegration of the Soviet Union, and the emergence of a unipolar world. Rise of new world powers like China and the European Union.

    United Nations and International Organizations: Role of the United Nations, IMF, World Bank, and WTO in global governance. India's role in these organizations and challenges to their functioning.

Media and Communication (Class 11 & 12 Syllabus)

Media and Communication is the study of how information is produced, transmitted, and received through various platforms like print, digital, radio, and television. It involves understanding the influence of media on public opinion, culture, and society, alongside developing practical skills in content creation, journalism, public relations, and digital media marketing.

Class 11 Media and Communication Syllabus:

    Introduction to Media: Overview of media industries and platforms such as print, radio, television, and digital. Study of the history and evolution of mass communication.

    Journalism Basics: Introduction to journalistic writing, news reporting, and editorial work. Focus on news ethics, media law, and investigative journalism.

    Media Technology: Study of the technological tools used in media production, including cameras, sound equipment, and editing software. Basics of photojournalism and videography.

    Media Literacy: Understanding how media influences public opinion, critical consumption of media content, and the role of media in shaping societal issues.

    Public Relations: Introduction to public relations (PR), corporate communication, and brand management. Basics of writing press releases and managing media events.

Class 12 Media and Communication Syllabus:

    Advertising and Marketing: Understanding media advertising strategies, market analysis, and consumer behavior. Focus on creating ad campaigns and understanding the advertising industry.

    Film and Television Production: Study of the processes involved in producing films, television shows, and documentaries. Training in screenwriting, directing, and production design.

    Digital Media and Social Media: Study of digital platforms like websites, blogs, and social media. Training in digital content creation, social media management, and online marketing.
