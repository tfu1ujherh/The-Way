RRB Group D Examination Overview
Exam Name RRB Group D
Conducting Body Railway Recruitment Board (RRB)
Exam Level National Level
Job Location All India
Educational Qualification 10th Pass / ITI
Category Government Jobs
Age Limit 18-33 years
Exam Mode Online (Computer-Based Test)
Selection Stages Computer-Based Test (CBT), Physical Efficiency Test (PET), Document Verification, Medical Examination
Exam Frequency Based on vacancies
Application Fee INR 500 (General Category), INR 250 (SC/ST/PWD/Female/Transgender/Minority/EWS)
Payscale Level 1 of 7th CPC Pay Matrix (INR 18,000-56,900)
Exam Purpose Recruitment of candidates for Group D positions in Indian Railways
Exam Language English, Hindi, and other regional languages
Exam Helpdesk No. Based on specific RRB region
Official Website http://www.rrbcdg.gov.in/
Selection Process Written Examination: Computer-Based Test (CBT), 90 minutes duration, Objective-type questions.
Physical Efficiency Test (PET): Male candidates - 35 kg weight for 100 meters in 2 minutes and run 1000 meters in 4.15 minutes; Female candidates - 20 kg weight for 100 meters in 2 minutes and run 1000 meters in 5.40 minutes.
Medical Examination: Must meet medical standards.
Document Verification: Verification of educational and other relevant documents.
Exam Pattern General Science: 25 questions
Mathematics: 25 questions
General Intelligence & Reasoning: 30 questions
General Awareness & Current Affairs: 20 questions
Total Marks: 100
Negative Marking: 1/3rd mark deducted for each wrong answer
Syllabus General Science: Physics, Chemistry, Life Sciences (10th level).
Mathematics: Number Systems, Decimals, Fractions, LCM & HCF, Ratio and Proportion, Percentage, Time and Distance, Time and Work, Algebra, Geometry.
General Intelligence & Reasoning: Analogies, Coding-Decoding, Decision Making, Statement-Conclusion, Syllogism, Alphabetical and Number Series.
General Awareness & Current Affairs: Current events of National and International Importance, Sports, Science & Technology, Indian Politics, Economics.
Application Process Notification: Check RRB's official website for updates.
Application Form: Available online on the respective RRB websites.
Application Fee: Payable online; exemptions available for reserved categories as per government rules.
Important Dates Notification Release: Announced on respective RRB websites.
Application Start Date: As per notification.
Application Deadline: Typically one month after the start date.
Exam Date: Announced in the notification.
Preparation Tips Understand the exam pattern, focus on syllabus topics, practice previous year question papers, stay updated with current affairs, and work on physical fitness for the PET.
Career Prospects Group D positions include Track Maintainer, Helper/Assistant in various technical departments, Porter, etc. Promotions and career growth are possible with experience and departmental exams.

Indian Army Soldier (General Duty) - Overview
Post Name Indian Army Soldier (General Duty)
Conducting Body Indian Army
Category Government Jobs (Defense)
Job Location Anywhere in India
Educational Qualification 10th Pass (Matriculation)
Age Limit 17.5 - 21 years
Selection Process Written Exam (Common Entrance Exam - CEE), Physical Fitness Test (PFT), Medical Examination, and Document Verification.
Exam Mode Online
Application Mode Online
Job Responsibilities General soldier duties include securing borders, participating in combat operations, disaster relief, and assisting during national emergencies.
Payscale INR 21,700 - 69,100 (with additional allowances such as dearness allowance, medical benefits, and more)
Physical Standards Height: 170 cm (varying slightly based on region and category)
Chest: Minimum 77 cm with a 5 cm expansion
Weight: Proportionate to height and age as per Army standards
Physical Fitness Test (PFT)
  • 1.6 km Run: Completed in less than 5 minutes 30 seconds
  • Pull-Ups: Minimum 6 pull-ups
  • Zig-Zag Balance Test
  • 9 Feet Ditch Jump
Medical Examination Medical standards as per Indian Army guidelines, including vision, hearing, and general health conditions.
Written Examination (CEE) Subjects: General Knowledge, General Science, and Mathematics
Question Type: Objective (Multiple Choice Questions)
Passing Marks: 35 out of 100 marks
Syllabus for Written Exam General Knowledge: Current affairs, History, Geography, Sports, etc.
General Science: Physics, Chemistry, and Biology (Class 10 level)
Mathematics: Arithmetic, Algebra, Geometry (Class 10 level)
Application Process Step 1: Visit the official Indian Army recruitment website: https://joinindianarmy.nic.in
Step 2: Register with a valid email ID and mobile number.
Step 3: Fill out the online application form, upload documents, and submit.
Step 4: Appear for the rally at the assigned recruitment center.
Important Documents
  • Matriculation certificate (for date of birth and educational qualification)
  • Domicile certificate
  • Caste certificate (if applicable)
  • Religion certificate (if applicable)
  • Character certificate
  • Unmarried certificate
Important Dates Recruitment rallies are conducted throughout the year. Candidates must check the Indian Army website for region-wise rally schedules.
Preparation Tips
  • Focus on physical fitness, especially running and strength training.
  • Study the exam syllabus thoroughly, focusing on the 10th standard level of General Science and Mathematics.
  • Stay updated on current affairs for the General Knowledge section.
  • Practice mock tests and previous years' question papers.
Career Prospects After selection as a Soldier (GD), one can rise through the ranks by gaining experience and performance. Promotions to Naik, Havildar, Naib Subedar, Subedar, and Subedar Major are possible over time. Additionally, Indian Army soldiers can apply for commission posts like Junior Commissioned Officer (JCO) through internal exams.
Official Website https://joinindianarmy.nic.in

Indian Navy Sailor (MR) - Overview
Post Name Indian Navy Sailor (Matric Recruit - MR)
Conducting Body Indian Navy
Category Government Jobs (Defense)
Job Location Anywhere in India or on board naval ships
Educational Qualification 10th Pass (Matriculation)
Age Limit 17 - 20 years
Selection Process Computer-Based Test (CBT), Physical Fitness Test (PFT), and Medical Examination.
Exam Mode Online (Computer-Based Test)
Application Mode Online
Job Responsibilities Sailors under MR perform duties related to Chef, Steward, and Hygienist onboard ships or submarines.
  • Chef: Prepare meals as per the menu and ensure ration management.
  • Steward: Serve food in officers' messes, handle accounting of funds, wine, and stores.
  • Hygienist: Maintain cleanliness of washrooms and other areas.
Payscale INR 21,700 - 69,100 (with additional allowances like DA, free medical, canteen benefits, and more)
Physical Standards Height: Minimum 157 cm
Chest: Minimum expansion of 5 cm
Weight: Proportionate to height and age
Physical Fitness Test (PFT)
  • 1.6 km Run: To be completed in 7 minutes
  • Squats (Uthak Baithak): 20 repetitions
  • Push-Ups: 10 repetitions
Medical Examination Candidates should be physically and mentally fit as per Navy standards. Eyesight must be 6/6 (Better Eye) and 6/9 (Worse Eye), without color blindness or night blindness.
Written Examination (CBT) Subjects: Science, Mathematics, General Knowledge
Duration: 30 minutes
Question Type: Objective (Multiple Choice Questions)
Marking Scheme: 1 mark for each correct answer; negative marking of 0.25 marks for incorrect answers.
Syllabus for Written Exam Science & Mathematics: Based on 10th standard syllabus
General Knowledge: Current affairs, Geography, History, Sports, and general information about India and the world.
Application Process Step 1: Visit the official Indian Navy website: https://joinindiannavy.gov.in
Step 2: Register with an email ID and mobile number.
Step 3: Fill out the online application form, upload documents, and submit the form.
Step 4: Download the admit card and appear for the CBT and further stages.
Important Documents
  • Matriculation certificate (for date of birth and educational qualification)
  • Domicile certificate
  • Caste certificate (if applicable)
  • Character certificate
  • Recent passport-sized photographs
Important Dates The recruitment process is conducted multiple times a year. Keep an eye on the official website for announcements.
Preparation Tips
  • Study the 10th standard syllabus for Science and Mathematics thoroughly.
  • Practice reasoning and general knowledge questions.
  • Prepare physically by focusing on endurance and strength training for the PFT.
  • Keep updated with current affairs.
  • Practice mock tests and go through previous years' question papers.
Career Prospects Sailors recruited under MR can rise through the ranks with experience. Promotions to Leading Seaman, Petty Officer, and Chief Petty Officer are possible. Further, Sailors may apply for commissioned officer roles through internal exams like the Artificer Apprentice (AA) or Senior Secondary Recruit (SSR) exams.
Official Website https://joinindiannavy.gov.in

Indian Air Force - Group ‘C’ Civilian - Overview
Post Name Indian Air Force Group ‘C’ Civilian
Conducting Body Indian Air Force
Category Government Jobs (Civilian Defense)
Job Location Across India (Air Force Stations and Units)
Educational Qualification Varies by post (e.g., 10th Pass, 12th Pass, Graduation, ITI).
Age Limit 18 - 25 years (Age relaxation as per government rules)
Selection Process Written Test, Skill/Practical/Physical Test (as applicable based on the post).
Exam Mode Offline (Pen and Paper Mode)
Application Mode Offline (by post)
Post Names Group ‘C’ posts include:
  • Multi-Tasking Staff (MTS)
  • Lower Division Clerk (LDC)
  • Storekeeper
  • Stenographer
  • Cook
  • Housekeeping Staff
  • Carpenter
  • Painter
  • Tailor
  • Mess Staff
  • Driver
  • Others (various technical and non-technical roles)
Payscale Pay level varies by post. Basic salary is typically between INR 18,000 - INR 25,500 per month, with allowances like DA, HRA, Transport Allowance, etc.
Physical Standards (if applicable) For some posts like MTS, Mess Staff, and Driver, physical standards may apply (e.g., fitness, driving skills for drivers).
Written Examination Pattern The written exam consists of objective-type questions:
  • General Intelligence & Reasoning
  • Numerical Aptitude
  • General English
  • General Awareness
Syllabus for Written Exam
  • General Intelligence & Reasoning: Analogies, Similarities, Differences, Space Visualization, Problem Solving, Analysis, Judgment, Decision Making, Visual Memory, Discrimination, Observation, Relationship Concepts, Arithmetical Reasoning, Verbal and Figure Classification.
  • Numerical Aptitude: Number Systems, Computation of Whole Numbers, Decimals, Fractions, and Relationship between Numbers, Fundamental Arithmetical Operations, Percentages, Ratios and Proportions, Averages, Interest, Profit and Loss, Discount, Time and Distance, Time and Work.
  • General English: Vocabulary, Grammar, Sentence Structure, Synonyms, Antonyms, and its correct usage.
  • General Awareness: Current Events, Indian History, Geography, Polity, Economics, and Sports.
Application Process Step 1: Candidates need to download the application form from the official Indian Air Force website.
Step 2: Fill the form and send it to the respective Air Force Station via post along with the required documents like educational certificates, identity proof, domicile, and recent photographs.
Step 3: Keep a copy of the application for future reference. There is no online application process for these posts.
Important Documents
  • Educational qualification certificates
  • Birth certificate
  • Domicile certificate
  • Passport-sized photographs
  • Caste certificate (if applicable)
  • Character certificate
Important Dates Recruitment notifications are released periodically on the official website and employment newspapers.
Preparation Tips
  • Study reasoning and aptitude concepts thoroughly.
  • Focus on current affairs for general awareness preparation.
  • Improve your basic English skills, including grammar and vocabulary.
  • Practice previous years' question papers to understand the exam pattern.
  • Time management during the exam is crucial, so practice mock tests regularly.
Career Prospects Group ‘C’ Civilian employees can be promoted to higher ranks based on performance and experience. Internal promotions and departmental exams are available for career growth within the Indian Air Force.
Official Website https://indianairforce.nic.in

  • Written Examination
  • Physical Standard Test (PST)

  • Birth certificate

  • Domicile certificate
  • Character certificate
  • Passport-sized photographs

Preparation Tips
  • Stay updated with current affairs, especially national events and state-specific news.
  • Focus on basic arithmetic and reasoning skills for the written test.
  • Practice physical fitness regularly to meet the PET standards.
  • Go through previous year question papers to understand the exam pattern.
  • Maintain a balance between written exam preparation and physical training.
Career Prospects Constables can be promoted to higher positions such as Head Constable, Assistant Sub-Inspector (ASI), Sub-Inspector (SI), and Inspector through internal exams and performance evaluations.
Official Website Each state has its own police recruitment website. For example:
  • Delhi Police
  • Uttar Pradesh Police
  • Tamil Nadu Police

For ABPM/Dak Sevak: INR 10,000 - INR 12,000 per month

  • For BPM: 4 to 5 hours per day

Step 5: Keep a printout of the application for future reference.

  • General/OBC/EWS Male: INR 100
  • SC/ST/PwD/Female: No fee
Important Documents
  • 10th pass certificate and mark sheet
  • Birth certificate or date of birth proof
  • Category certificate (if applicable)
  • Domicile certificate
  • Passport-sized photographs
  • Other relevant documents as per the notification
Important Dates Recruitment notifications are released periodically based on postal circle vacancies.
Preparation Tips
  • Ensure all documents are ready for uploading, especially the 10th-grade mark sheet.
  • Focus on achieving good grades in the 10th standard, as selection is purely merit-based.
  • Stay updated on recruitment notifications from the respective postal circles.
Career Prospects
  • After gaining experience, GDS employees can be promoted to other roles within the Postal Department through internal exams.
  • Opportunities for promotions to Postmaster, Supervisor, or higher administrative roles.
